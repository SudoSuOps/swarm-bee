Why Datasets
Why is training data the real asset?

Models depreciate. Data appreciates. Every foundation model released makes the last one obsolete — but the training data that makes a model useful in a specific domain only gets more valuable over time.

A model is a delivery vehicle. The data is the product. If you own the data, you can retrain on every new architecture that drops. If you only own the model, you're one release cycle from irrelevance.

Scale AI charges $100 per training example. We have 828,000+ at $0.25/pair. The data is the moat.
What makes S&B data different from public datasets?

Three things: source, verification, and provenance.

  • Source: 18 authoritative medical textbooks (Harrison's, Schwartz's, Robbins, Gray's, etc). Not scraped from Reddit, not generated by ChatGPT, not pulled from forums.
  • Verification: Every pair passes a 4-stage quality gate with Chain-of-Verification by a 235B parameter model (Qwen3-235B). 93.6% conversion rate. The pairs that don't pass get rewritten until they do.
  • Provenance: Every pair is MD5-fingerprinted and anchored to Hedera blockchain. You can verify the origin, timestamp, and integrity of any pair in the vault. Try that with MedQA.

We scored 30/30 on SFT suitability — the highest score of any medical dataset benchmarked. That's not marketing. That's measured.

Why 59 medical specialties instead of one deep vertical?

Because the buyer decides what they need, not us. A cardiology startup needs cardiology. A hospital system needs everything. A researcher needs the specialty their grant is focused on.

Broad inventory lets us serve every buyer that walks in the door. Then we go deep on demand — someone submits an RFP for 10,000 pediatric cardiology pairs, we grind it. The breadth is the storefront. The depth is the custom work.

How do you verify 828,000+ pairs?

Chain-of-Verification (CoVe). Every pair goes through:

  • Stage 1: Generation from authoritative source material using clinical templates
  • Stage 2: Structural validation (format, completeness, deduplication)
  • Stage 3: CoVe verification by Qwen3-235B — the verifier checks factual accuracy, clinical reasoning, and answer completeness
  • Stage 4: Pairs that FLAG get rewritten and re-verified. 42% of pairs go through at least one rewrite cycle.

57% pass on first verification. 93.6% pass after rewrite. The remaining 6.4% are discarded. Zero garbage makes it to the vault.

Why Compute
Why dedicated hardware instead of cloud?

Cloud GPU is a time-share. You share the hardware, you share the risk, and your data transits through someone else's network. That's fine for a weekend hackathon. It's unacceptable for clinical AI.

Dedicated means:

  • Your rig. Nobody else touches it. No noisy neighbors.
  • Your data never leaves the box. Not in transit, not at rest, not ever.
  • Wallet-gated API — cryptographic proof that only your systems can query your model.
  • 72-hour hardware replacement SLA — not a ticket in a queue, a swap.

AWS charges $32/hr for an A100. That's $23,000/month. We deliver 96GB of dedicated VRAM for $3,500/month with an SLA and no data leaving your control.

What's a spec build?

In commercial real estate, a spec build is a property built on speculation — you construct it before you have a tenant, betting that the market will fill it. The buyer enters at a higher cap rate (lower price) because the building isn't stabilized yet. They backfill the tenant, stabilize the NOI, and the value doubles.

S&B spec build, same economics:

  • Entry at $500K (10% cap rate) — the rig is built, the data is flowing, but there's no tenant yet
  • You backfill the tenant (find the hospital, the startup, the enterprise buyer)
  • Stabilized at $1M (5% cap rate) — 2x on your entry
  • 10-year term, 5% annual NOI bumps, 120K new pairs/year as deferred maintenance
You can't sell a spec build at a stabilized cap rate. The value-add is the whole point. That's where the 2x lives.

Why Sovereign
What does "sovereign" actually mean?

Three guarantees:

  • Your data never leaves your rack. Not for training, not for inference, not for logging. The model lives on your hardware or hardware dedicated to you. No cloud hop.
  • Your model is yours. We fine-tune it on your data, your specialty, your use case. The weights belong to you. We can't use them, sell them, or train on them.
  • Your access is cryptographic. Wallet-gated API means a private key controls who queries the model. Not a username and password. A key pair. Verifiable on Hedera.

Sovereign isn't a marketing word. It's an architecture decision. Every piece of the stack — data, model, hardware, access — is single-tenant by design.

Why does sovereignty matter for healthcare?

Because a hospital CIO who sends patient-adjacent data to a cloud API is one breach away from a career-ending headline.

HIPAA doesn't care about your vendor's SOC 2 report. It cares about whether protected health information left your control. With sovereign deployment, it never does. The model runs in your data center. The inference happens on your network. The logs stay on your box.

That's not a compliance checkbox. That's a fundamental architecture guarantee. Your compliance officer doesn't need to review our security posture because your data never touches our systems.

Why Hedera for on-chain provenance?

Because provenance without proof is just a promise. And promises don't survive audits.

  • Hedera Consensus Service (HCS): Every agent action, every sale, every data delivery is logged to a tamper-proof, timestamped topic. You can independently verify any claim we make.
  • Hedera Token Service (HTS): Every data bundle, every model, every term sheet is an NFT with on-chain metadata. The provenance travels with the asset.
  • Why not Ethereum/Base? We started there. Genesis block is on ETH L1. But Hedera is enterprise-grade: $0.0001 per transaction, 3-second finality, no MEV, no gas wars. It's where the adults build.
2,660 NFTs on Hedera mainnet. 47 HCS messages and counting. Every pair fingerprinted. Every delivery anchored. Provenance, not promises.

That's the last mile. The 14B model on a $3,500/month rig in a hospital's server room, answering cardiology questions at 34 tokens per second with zero data leakage. That's what we deliver. That's where the margin is.

The Problem with Today's AI

Most AI today lives in the cloud.

  • Expensive
  • Slow
  • Opaque
  • Dependent on third-party APIs
  • Difficult to control or audit

For many industries, this creates real friction:

  • Healthcare and legal privacy concerns
  • Industrial uptime requirements
  • Unreliable connectivity
  • Cost and latency at scale
AI needs a last-mile architecture.
What "Last-Mile AI" Means

In logistics, the hardest part is the last mile to the customer.

In AI, the hardest part is the last mile to the user and their data.

Swarm-LLM is built to solve that.

Instead of one giant model in the cloud, we run a hierarchy of models that work together.

The Swarm Model Hierarchy

This is the heart of the platform.

4B → 8B → 14B → 32B

Each model has a role.

4B The Edge Router
Fast. Local. Always on.

Runs on BeeMini / Zima edge devices.

  • Voice interface
  • Intent detection
  • Routing requests
  • Quick answers
  • Privacy-first local processing

This model is your AI front desk.

8B The Assistant Layer
Everyday task engine.
  • Drafting
  • Summarization
  • Light reasoning
  • Workflow automation
  • Business tasks

Fast enough to run locally or on a nearby node.

14B The Specialist Layer
Domain intelligence.

Dedicated specialists:

  • SwarmMed — medical
  • SwarmAviation — aviation
  • SwarmCRE — real estate
  • SwarmLegal — legal
  • SwarmOps — IT / DevOps

These models do the real work.

32B The Research Brain
Deep thinking + synthesis.

Used when tasks require:

  • Multi-document analysis
  • Complex planning
  • Long-context reasoning
  • Incident and research workflows

This runs on rack-scale hardware.

235B The Verifier
Quality control.
  • Dataset validation
  • Verification pipelines
  • Quality assurance

This ensures the system improves over time.

The BeeMini Edge Node

Small device. Big role.

BeeMini is the local gateway to the Swarm. Runs 24/7 using low power and connects users to the model hierarchy.
